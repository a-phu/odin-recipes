Green Papaya Salad

Description

This Thai salad – called Som Tum – is a riot of fresh colours and crunchy textures, brought to life with a signature sour-sweet-savoury dressing spiked with a whack of fresh chilli.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp garlic
  • 6 bird eye chillies
  • 6 tbsp dried shrimp
  • 1 cup palm sugar
  • 1/2 cup lime juice
  • 1 cup roasted peanuts
  • 20 snake beans
  • 3 cups grape tomato
  • 500g / 4 cups green papaya
  • 1/2 cup Thai basil leaves

Steps

  1. Crush peanuts: Place peanuts in a mortar and pestle. Pound lightly to break them up into largish pieces, not into powder. Transfer to bowl.
  2. Garlic and chilli paste: Place garlic and chilli in the mortar. Pound into a paste. Add shrimp and pound to crush them – no need to grind them to a paste.
  3. Dressing: Stir in palm sugar, lime and fish sauce until sugar dissolves. Pour Dressing into a large bowl.
  4. Bruise snake beans: Add snake beans to mortar (in batches if needed). Pound to bruise, split and soften (they are raw, so they need to be bashed to soften). Add to Dressing.
  5. Crush tomato: Grab handfuls of tomato, crush with your hands then add into the bowl.
  6. Add papaya: Add papaya and 3/4 of the peanuts. Toss well with 2 wooden spoons or tongs.
  7. Serve immediately.
